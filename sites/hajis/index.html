Our story

Three decades on Amsterdam Avenue.

For more than thirty years, Hajis Famous Deli has been a fixture of Morningside Heights — the kind of place where Columbia undergrads, night-shift workers, and neighborhood regulars all wind up at the same counter. The chopped cheese wasn’t just perfected here; it was invented here. The New York Times came uptown to write about it in 2016, and the food shows have been finding their way to our door ever since.

Alongside the original chopped cheese you’ll find signature heroes built on a griddle we’ve been running for decades, breakfast sandwiches from six in the morning, hand-rolled omelettes, fresh-pressed juices, and a build-your-own sandwich board that can put almost anything on a roll. We also cater — fresh juices, sandwich platters, and fruit trays for offices, classrooms, and events around the neighborhood.

Lately the counter has grown to include Dubai-style desserts, house protein bars, yogurt-and-oat breakfast bowls, and fresh ginger shots. We stay open late because the neighborhood asks us to — whether you’re coming off a shift, grinding through finals week, or just hungry at an hour when most kitchens have closed.
